Program Overview

The RESTORE Them Project 3-Day Peer Workforce Development Program prepares individuals with lived experience in mental health or substance use recovery to become certified peer support professionals — grounded in RESTORE's mission of restoring dignity through lived experience, faith, and transformative relationship.

Why Peer Workforce Development?

Peer workforce development programs train individuals with lived experience to become certified professionals who support others navigating recovery. Research consistently shows that peer support specialists reduce hospital admissions, lower emergency department visits, and build trust in communities clinical staff often cannot reach.


In Texas, certifications like the Certified Recovery Peer Specialist (CRPS) and Peer Recovery Supporter (PRS) create formal career pathways — from community outreach and criminal justice settings to hospitals and peer-run organizations. This program contributes toward those certification hours.

4 Program Phases

  • Foundation — Assessment, orientation & spiritual grounding
  • Core Recovery — Counseling, support groups & life skills
  • Transition & Integration — Community re-entry & job readiness
  • Aftercare & Ongoing Support — Mentorship & relapse prevention

Key Training Components

  • Certification Training — Aligned with Texas CRPS & PRS requirements
  • Skill Development — Active listening, crisis response, ethical decision-making
  • Professionalization — Resume writing, interview prep, workplace documentation
  • Career Paths — Mentorship, supervision, and career ladder orientation
  • Faith Integration — Christ-centered, Bible-based 12-step principles
  • Holistic Planning — Physical, emotional, mental, and spiritual health

1A What Is Peer Support? Roles, Scope & Ethics ▼
P2Person-Driven P6Peer Support P9Respect

Participants explore the formal definition of peer support, the difference between peer specialists and clinical providers, and the ethical framework governing peer work in Texas.

  • Define Certified Recovery Peer Specialist (CRPS) and Peer Recovery Supporter (PRS)
  • Review Texas HHSC peer support service definitions and settings: hospitals, community outreach, criminal justice, peer-run organizations
  • RESTORE's Person-Driven principle — empowering individuals to take charge of their own recovery
  • Scope of practice: what peers do and do not do — maintaining safe, ethical role clarity
  • Ethics case study: confidentiality, dual relationships, and professional limits
Facilitator Note: Normalize the mix of lived experience and professional skill — both are equally required. Reference Texas HHS resources and PeerHubTX (centralizedtraining.com/peerhubtx) for certification details.
1B My Story Is My Credential: Recovery Narrative Workshop ▼
P1Faith-Based P6Peer Support P7Relational

Personal narrative is the defining professional tool of peer support. This workshop guides participants through the RESTORE Story Framework.

The RESTORE Story Framework

  • BEFORE — Who were you before your turning point? Humanize, do not dramatize.
  • DURING — What was recovery like? What helped? What hurt?
  • AFTER — Where are you now? What has changed?
  • HOPE — What do you want others to know is possible?

Format: 3–5 minutes per person. Partners practice active listening. Debrief as full group.

Module Details

2A Active Listening, Motivational Interviewing & Empathy Skills ▼
P6Peer SupportP7RelationalP2Person-Driven

The OARS framework from Motivational Interviewing helps participants engage others without judgment, advice-giving, or clinical language.

The OARS Framework

  • O — Open-Ended Questions: Invite the person to tell their story in their own words
  • A — Affirmations: Recognize strengths, not just struggles
  • R — Reflective Listening: Mirror back what you hear without interpreting or advising
  • S — Summarizing: Bring the conversation together and invite correction

Format: Triads — Speaker / Listener / Observer. Rotate roles. Observer uses OARS checklist. Debrief after each round.

3B Texas CRPS/PRS Certification Pathway ▼
P8Strengths/ResponsibilityP2Person-Driven

Texas CRPS Pathway Overview

  • Eligibility: Self-identify as a person in recovery (mental health or substance use)
  • Training Hours: Complete HHSC-approved training — this program contributes toward required totals
  • Supervised Work: Documented peer support hours under a qualified supervisor
  • Exam: Pass the Texas CRPS or PRS certification examination
  • Renewal: Continuing education and ethics requirements

Resources: Texas HHS (hhs.texas.gov) | PeerHubTX (centralizedtraining.com/peerhubtx) | Thrive Peer Recovery (thrivepeersupport.com)

Appendices

A — Facilitator Guidelines

  • Lead from lived experience — your story is your authority in this room
  • Hold "Nothing About Us Without Us" in every design decision and facilitation choice
  • Maintain trauma-informed facilitation: offer movement breaks, honor emotional responses, never force sharing
  • Faith integration is central; individual participation in spiritual elements is always voluntary and honored either way
  • Use inclusive language that honors diverse recovery pathways, cultural backgrounds, and faith traditions
  • Co-facilitate whenever possible — peer and professional together models the relationship
  • Document participant hours carefully to support CRPS/PRS certification applications
